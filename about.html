About This Project

Since 2015, my dad and I have spent much of our time living in different time zones. Even with the jet lag and the challenges of work schedules, he used to visit me in the United States every month. But after the pandemic, flights between China and the U.S. became far less frequent. Now, he can only make the trip every two or three months, and each visit lasts just about two weeks. Like many families separated by distance, we’ve had to find ways to stay connected despite being thousands of miles apart.

This project is an exploration on how our lives play out in parallel on opposite sides of the Pacific Ocean. It’s a reflection of time, distance, and the connections that endure despite these separations. By visualizing our daily rhythms and moments that intersect, I hope to create something that not only captures our story, but also resonates with others who’ve experienced the challenge of staying close across time zones.
